# Journal

Append-only practice log. One paragraph per session. No structure beyond the date and topic.

!!! tip "Why write here"
    The brain compresses learning across many sessions. A journal is how you notice patterns ("every time I sight-read after coffee I'm faster" / "the third home-design iteration always fixes the second one's mistake") that you'd otherwise miss. **Five minutes of writing now beats thirty minutes of regret later.**

!!! note "How to write entries"
    Be specific. "Practiced piano" is useless. "20 min: drilled C, G, D major scales hands-together at 80 BPM; D still trips on right-hand 4-3 crossing; assigned to tomorrow" is gold.

---

## Format

```
## YYYY-MM-DD — <topic>

<duration>: <what you did>. <what you noticed>. <next step or open question>.
```

Multiple entries per day are fine — one per session, or one per topic.

---

## Weekly review (every Sunday)

Skim the past week's entries. Ask:

- What did I actually do, vs. what I planned (in [now.md](./now.md))?
- Did any entry note a pattern, blocker, or question I should act on?
- Anything to move into a topic's `practice-schedule.md` or onto next week's commitments?

Then update `now.md` for the coming week.
